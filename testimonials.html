Stories from the practice alongside reflections from clients — what this work looks like from both sides.

From the Practitioner's Perspective

Stories that stay with me

"I haven't thought about — or craved — sugar for several days now. How weird is that?"

Christina came to me while her husband was in the final stages of a chronic illness. A mother, a professional, a caretaker — holding a lot on her own. She had started using sugar to cope. She didn't know how to shift it.

We began where we always begin — with what was present in the body. As we worked, something older surfaced: her relationship with a critical mother, and the emotional imprint of growing up in that dynamic.

We stayed with it gently. Her system began to shift as we worked with what she was holding and created a different internal experience of safety.

A few days later: "I haven't thought about — or craved — sugar for several days now. How weird is that?"

Weeks later she placed one small piece of candy in a bowl on her counter. Not to eat it — as a reminder of how far she'd come.

— Christina, client

"That sounds like forgiveness."

Delilah came months after an injury that had left her system shaken — tension, migraines, memory issues, low mood. After one session, her migraines eased significantly. She slept deeply for the first time in months.

A couple of weeks later she brought up something she hadn't shared before. Years ago, during a medical procedure, she hadn't felt safe. That experience had stayed in her body long after.

In session it surfaced while we were working near an area that still held tension. We paused and stayed with what was present. I explained that energetic imprints from difficult experiences can remain in the body — and that with permission, they can be released.

She paused and said:

"That sounds like forgiveness."

I smiled. "Sometimes it is. But it doesn't require forgiveness. It simply asks: do you want to keep carrying this? If not, we can let it go."

She said, absolutely.

That week she felt effervescent. Her creativity began returning. A month later — the scar that had always brought the memory back with it was fading.

— Delilah, client

"Such a massive turn of events! It feels like a new job this month. I'm really enjoying work!"

Carrie called one morning when she was really sick — weak, nauseous, vomiting — and dreading going to work more than she ever had. Her boss had been making the environment genuinely toxic for a long time, and her body was telling her it had had enough.

I didn't ask her to come to me. I came to her. We sat in her kitchen and talked through everything that was going on, and together we started mapping out the beliefs she had been carrying and the energetic imprints the situation had left behind. Then I did Chi Nei Tsang — gentle abdominal work that had first brought us together — which helped settle and ground what we were working on.

I never told her what was wrong with her or claimed to have all the answers. We figured it out together. After the session she worked on affirmations and kept coming back to a vision of how she wanted things to feel.

The situation at work resolved in a way neither of us could have predicted. In a very good way. She went from dreading every morning to genuinely loving her job again.

"Something moved that day. I'm certain of it."

— Carrie L., client

"Eventually the comical gator just faded away."

In the 1950s, when Judy was around 4 or 5, she was nearly taken by alligators at a park in Florida. Her family was visiting, and while her parents talked with friends she knelt down near the pit, went through the fence, and stuck her arm in to pet the gators. The top one shot straight up and missed her hand by inches.

For decades afterward, she couldn't lie in bed on her stomach with her arm over the edge without that image flooding back.

She told me the story and I asked if she'd like to do a Theta Healing session to release it. She wholeheartedly said yes.

Afterward, when the image returned — the alligator appeared almost comical. And then, eventually, it faded away entirely.

"I am deeply grateful for Gwen's healing work and would recommend it to others needing help."

— Judy H.

"I'm amazed at how good my knees are feeling now."

He came to me with chronic knee pain — something that hadn't been a problem before, but a move to a new city had brought new conditions. Hot outside, air conditioning running hard at work all day. The constant swing between temperatures was landing in his knees.

We worked together weekly for six weeks. Session by session, his knees began to respond — the inflammation settling, the body finding its footing in the new environment.

He still comes in for regular tune-ups. Sometimes twice a month, sometimes once. The acute issue has resolved, and what continues now is something broader — an ongoing investment in his overall wellbeing.

"I come back for regular tune-ups now. The ongoing work has been really beneficial to my overall wellbeing."

— ID
