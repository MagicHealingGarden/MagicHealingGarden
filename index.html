The Work in Practice

What becomes possible

"I haven't thought about — or craved — sugar for several days now. How weird is that?"

Christina came to me while her husband was in the final stages of a chronic illness. A mother, a professional, a caretaker — holding a lot on her own. She had started using sugar to cope. She didn't know how to shift it.

We began where we always begin — with what was present in the body. As we worked, something older surfaced: her relationship with a critical mother, and the emotional imprint of growing up in that dynamic.

We stayed with it gently. Her system began to shift as we worked with what she was holding and created a different internal experience of safety.

A few days later: "I haven't thought about — or craved — sugar for several days now. How weird is that?"

Weeks later she placed one small piece of candy in a bowl on her counter. Not to eat it — as a reminder of how far she'd come.

"That sounds like forgiveness."

Elena came months after an injury that had left her system shaken — tension, migraines, memory issues, low mood. After one session, her migraines eased significantly. She slept deeply for the first time in months.

A couple of weeks later she brought up something she hadn't shared before. Years ago, during a medical procedure, she hadn't felt safe. That experience had stayed in her body long after.

In session it surfaced while we were working near an area that still held tension. We paused and stayed with what was present. I explained that energetic imprints from difficult experiences can remain in the body — and that with permission, they can be released.

She paused and said:

"That sounds like forgiveness."

I smiled. "Sometimes it is. But it doesn't require forgiveness. It simply asks: do you want to keep carrying this? If not, we can let it go."

She said, absolutely.

That week she felt effervescent. Her creativity began returning. A month later — the scar that had always brought the memory back with it was fading.

You Might Be My People

I work with people who give

"If you take care of everyone else first — this is for you."

When I look back at the people I've done my deepest work with, almost all of them share something: they give generously, they hold space for others, and their own needs have been quietly going unmet for a long time.

Therapists Bodyworkers Acupuncturists Massage Therapists Energy Healers Teachers Educators Social Workers Nurses & Caregivers Movement Practitioners Activists Parents Executives & Leaders Managers & Team Leaders Lawyers Librarians Caterers & Private Chefs

Whether or not you call yourself a healer — if you spend your days holding space for other people's pain, growth, and needs, your system absorbs what they release. You don't need a title for this to apply to you. You just need to recognize yourself in it.

You deserve the same quality of care you offer everyone else. Let someone tend to you for a change.

Modalities

How we work together

My sessions draw from multiple lineages — not because I collected modalities, but because each one found me when I needed it and proved itself in practice. I use what works, in the combination that's right for you.

Before every session I center myself and set intention — for your highest good and the highest good of everyone connected to you. You arrive into a space that has already been prepared for you.

🌿
Body-Centered Healing
Drawing from Chinese medicine, Zen Shiatsu, Chi Nei Tsang, and acupressure — we work with the body as an intelligent system that holds memory, story, and unfinished business. The body knows what happened. We help it complete the cycle.
✨
Theta Healing & Energy Work
A meditation-based healing modality that works at the level of belief and energetic memory. Old shocks, inherited patterns, energy that was never yours to carry — with your permission, these can be released. The results often surprise people.
🌸
Trauma-Informed Care
Every session is held with an understanding that the body protects itself — and that those protections made sense once. We work with your nervous system, not against it. Nothing is forced. Everything requires your yes.
💧
Flower Essence Therapy
Gentle vibrational medicine that supports emotional and energetic shifts between sessions. Often the quiet thread that holds everything else together.
🌬️
Guided Breathwork
Sometimes the body isn't ready for touch. Breathwork offers a way in through the breath itself — calming the nervous system, moving stagnant energy, restoring ease and strength. Sessions are gentle, guided, and entirely non-invasive.
