Guided
Breathwork

Breath is the one thing the body does both automatically and consciously. Which makes it one of the most direct ways in.

I offer breathwork as its own complete modality — not as a warm-up or add-on, but as a full session in its own right. Some of the people I work with come specifically for breathwork and nothing else. They may not want touch, may not be ready for it, or simply find that this is what their system responds to most. That is a completely valid place to begin — or to stay.

I also weave breathwork into sessions when the body needs a different entry point. Sometimes the system isn't ready for hands-on work. Sometimes we've done energetic work and the breath is what integrates it. The breath is flexible in a way that touch sometimes isn't — it can go where hands can't, working with the nervous system directly to calm what has been activated and move what has been stuck.

Sessions are gentle, guided, and entirely non-invasive. You don't need any prior experience. You just need to be willing to breathe a little more intentionally than usual.

Why breath

"The breath is always available. It is the body's own built-in tool for regulation — and sometimes it just needs someone to guide it."

Breathwork can be offered as a standalone session or woven into a broader session that includes bodywork or energy work. If you're not sure where to start, this is often a gentle and accessible first step.

When it's the right tool

Sometimes the body needs
a different way in

There are moments when the body is simply not ready for touch. Trauma can make physical contact feel threatening — even when a person deeply wants to heal. For these clients, breathwork offers a pathway that the body can accept. It is non-invasive, entirely within their control, and surprisingly effective at reaching what has been held.

There are also moments mid-session when the work needs to shift. We've done something significant — released a pattern, moved something that had been stuck — and the system needs to settle and integrate. The breath is what does that. It signals to the nervous system: the work is complete. You can rest now.

And sometimes people simply come to breathwork because they want to. Because they've never had a practice of breathing consciously and they're curious what it opens. That curiosity is enough.

What to Expect

The Session

We begin with a brief check-in — where you are today, what's present in the body, what you're hoping to work with. This grounds the session and helps me understand what approach will serve you best.

You'll be in a comfortable supported position — lying down or seated, depending on what feels right. I guide the breath gently, offering simple instructions and cues as we go. There's nothing to perform or achieve. The breath does the work. Your job is simply to follow.

Sessions are quiet, unhurried, and held with care. Nothing is forced. Everything at your pace. If at any point something feels like too much, we slow down or stop. You are always in the lead.

Breathwork can be offered as a standalone hour or woven into a session that includes other modalities. Many people find that beginning with breathwork — settling the system before doing deeper work — makes the rest of the session more accessible.

  • Duration

    Approximately one hour as a standalone session. Can also be woven into a longer session alongside bodywork or energy work.

  • What to Wear

    Comfortable, loose clothing. You'll be lying down or seated for most of the session.

  • Experience Required

    None. If you can breathe, you can do this. I guide you through everything.

  • After the Session

    Drink plenty of water. Give yourself time to rest and integrate if you can. People often feel calmer, clearer, and more present in their bodies afterward.
