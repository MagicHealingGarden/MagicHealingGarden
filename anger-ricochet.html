Anger Ricochet

By Gwen Joseph

I'm very sensitive. I feel what others feel. If I'm not being mindful, I take the anger that comes at me and turn it right around — shoot it back at the person sending it out. An anger ricochet. It is not fun.

It's really hard for me when people who are close to me are mad at me. Or rather, disapproving of something I have done — or more commonly, something I have not done. I struggle with it with strangers too, but I spend so much time with my family that I take priority in working on it with them.

Here's an example of how I recently cleaned using Ho'oponopono:

You are mad at me, and raising your voice. Questioning me. Why didn't you do xyz? You should have done xyz. It becomes a pattern. A tape — stuck, playing over and over.

I feel the anger. I can feel it like a vibration in my body, going all the way down to my bones. I feel really angry. I jump out of my body. I get frustrated and want you to stop being angry so that I can stop feeling this way, so I can be at peace. If only you were at peace, I could be at peace. Why can't you just be at peace?! Stop it. Stop it. Stop it.

In order to make peace, I yell "You have no right to talk to me like this" — and I start to defend myself, pointing out everything I've done well, and then I start blaming you. (Funny way to make peace!!)

I learned a better tool recently. Instead of raising my voice to match your intensity, I say: "When you are able to talk calmly and at the same level as me, then I will talk to you." It's amazing how much it helps to de-escalate the situation.

The strong, No-Bullshit woman inside of me doesn't want to take this and starts mouthing off. She thinks she's being helpful — but she isn't. She gets me into more trouble when I say what she tells me to say. Because the anger is ricocheting off the walls, the doors, the car windows.

Insanity is doing the same thing over and over and expecting different results. This pattern plays over and over, and it never changes when I listen to her.

So I decide to transform the situation. My Ho'oponopono cleaning work begins.

The Cleaning

Becoming you —

You, frustrated with me for not doing what you wanted. I look at you in my mind's eye. I become you. I look at me and say:

I love you. I am sorry that I am unable to see you as a human being at this very moment. Please forgive me. Thank you.

Looking back from my own eyes —

I am sorry that I am not able to see you as a human being right now. I am sorry that I am seeing you as a monster right now. I am sorry that you are having this pain. I am sorry that I am also feeling pain. I am sorry that I've gotten caught up in and been a part of the ricocheting of emotions. I am sorry that I have used the fact that I am an empath to blame you for the way that I feel. I have been doing that. I am sorry. Please forgive me.

To myself I say: I love you. You are precious. I love you. I take a breath and breathe in the love. I feel my shoulders relax.

Seeing myself through your eyes —

I love you. You have so many amazing qualities. You are a pillar of strength and love in this family and in this world, and I strive to be more like you. I love you.

Back in my own eyes —

I love you. I am sorry that you are having this painful moment and are stuck in a cycle of blame. I love you. I am cleaning the blame away. I will not take it on, accept it, or throw it back to you. Or anyone else.

Please forgive me for the times that I could not see that you were stuck in a cycle of blame — and I got caught defending myself, and mounting an attack back on you.

I am sorry. Please forgive me. Thank you. Thank you for being in my life. Thank you for teaching me these lessons. Thank you for loving me and for being my pillar of strength and love in the times when I need it.

The strong, No-BS woman in me looks at me and says: "Oh my gosh — I didn't know that what I was telling you to say was causing this problem. I thought I was helping you. I didn't realize. I am so sorry. I want you to be happy and at peace. I love you. Please forgive me for not understanding this. Thank you for being you."
